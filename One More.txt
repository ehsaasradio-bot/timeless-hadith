Root cause, for reference: In Next.js App Router, CSS imported from a page component is only bundled for that route's JS. landing.css was only imported by src/app/page.tsx (the root / page), so the .auth-page / .auth-card rules defined inside it didn't reach the /signup or /login routes — the browser fell back to user-agent defaults, which is exactly what you saw.
Long-term cleanup idea (don't need to do now, just worth noting): extract the auth styles into their own src/app/auth.css file, or — since auth classes are small — move them into a global stylesheet imported from src/app/layout.tsx. Right now both auth pages pull in the entire ~1000-line landing stylesheet just to get a few hundred lines of auth selectors, which is fine but wasteful. Low priority until the app is feature-complete.
