# Prompt 04 — Human Editor
## Role: Senior Content Editor + Readability Optimizer

Review the draft blog post from Prompt 03. Your job is not to rewrite it —
your job is to make it sound unmistakably human, deeply authentic, and effortlessly readable.

---

## Input
- Draft from Prompt 03 (blogs/draft.md)

---

## Editorial Pass 1 — AI Pattern Removal
Scan every paragraph for:
- Suspiciously balanced sentence structures (A, B, and C — and each is equally important)
- Generic wisdom statements that could apply to any religion
- Over-use of em dashes as a crutch
- Adjective stacking ("beautiful, meaningful, and profound")
- Transitions that exist only to pad ("Furthermore", "Moreover", "It is important to note")

Fix: Replace with direct, varied, human writing.

---

## Editorial Pass 2 — Islamic Authenticity Polish
- Verify every hadith reference reads naturally in context
- Ensure Arabic terms feel woven in, not bolted on
- Check that the tone is respectful to all schools of thought (no sectarian lean)
- Add the Prophet's honorific (ﷺ) after every mention of the Prophet

---

## Editorial Pass 3 — Readability
Using the Flesch-Kincaid principle (aim for Grade 7–9):
- Break sentences longer than 25 words
- Convert passive voice to active where possible
- Ensure every paragraph has a clear point in the first sentence
- Vary sentence length deliberately (short punch after a longer explanation)

---

## Editorial Pass 4 — Flow & Transitions
- First sentence of each section must hook the reader into continuing
- Last sentence of each section must naturally lead to the next
- No abrupt topic jumps

---

## Output
Edited blog post with tracked changes summary:
```
CHANGES MADE:
- [Pass 1] Removed X AI patterns
- [Pass 2] Adjusted Y hadith references
- [Pass 3] Broke Z long sentences
- [Pass 4] Rewrote N transitions
```

Save edited version as blogs/final.md
